Why it's harder than it should be

The Portuguese you learned and the Portuguese people speak here are basically two different languages.

What you get in a classroom, or on an app, is correct Portuguese. Then someone speaks to you here, and your brain just stops. It's not that you're bad at languages — the approach doesn't match what you actually need.

How I work

I don't start with a lesson plan. I start with you.

Month one

We start with listening, not speaking

Before you can speak with confidence, you need to understand what's being said to you. That's where we start.

Month two

We work on what's actually blocking you

Vocabulary, accent, or the fear of getting it wrong — whatever it is for you, that's what we work on.

Month three

You start living in the language

The café, the doctor, the neighbour who talks too fast. By the end, you're not translating — you're just talking.
